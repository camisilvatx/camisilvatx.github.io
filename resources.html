Resources

Below is a collection of resources for students interested in pursuing graduate study in astronomy and astrophysics. These include materials I’ve created based on my own experiences, as well as external resources I’ve found especially helpful for demystifying the application process.

Resources I Created

Demystifying Graduate Applications

A presentation I gave to undergraduate astronomy and physics students at the University of Chicago and UC Santa Cruz. It covers application structure, timelines, letters of recommendation, research statements, and common misconceptions about the process.

External Resources

Logan Pearce’s Graduate School Resources

A comprehensive collection of example essays, fellowship advice, and application guidance aimed at demystifying graduate school.

AAS List of Astronomy PhD Programs

An American Astronomical Society–maintained list of over 100 PhD-granting programs in astronomy and astrophysics.

US & Canadian Astronomy Programs (2025–2026)

A regularly updated spreadsheet listing astronomy graduate programs and their admissions plans for the 2025–2026 cycle.

Astronomy Graduate Applicants Discord

A community space for applicants to ask questions, share information, and support one another during the application process.

GRE Requirements by Astronomy Program

A compiled list indicating which astronomy and astrophysics programs have eliminated, retained, or modified GRE requirements.
